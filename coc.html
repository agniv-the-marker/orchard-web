`code of conduct`

At [orchard] events we are dedicated to providing a welcoming and safe experience for all. We ask that you hold respect for the space and the people within it.

Hosts may ask anyone violating the code of conduct to leave the session and/or discord server. If you’ve violated the code of conduct and been asked to leave, you must obtain written approval (ox.orchard@gmail.com) from [orchard]’s host team prior to joining us for any future sessions or [orchard] events. Failure to obtain approval is a violation of the code of conduct.

Respecting each other

  • ‘Host mindset’: if you see someone alone, go talk to them! Often, this looks like someone who is physically in a group but is not really engaging in a conversation. Try to ask questions and include them!
  • Be thoughtful in how you communicate. People at our sessions come from all different backgrounds and are looking to learn -- be ready to explain concepts and terms at varying levels of complexity and avoid exclusive jargon.
  • Don’t interrupt people when they’re speaking. Ask positive or constructive questions during demos.

    • Respecting the space

    • Clean up after yourself
    • Follow the venue-specific rules that are set out at the beginning of each session (particularly in the art room)

    Harassment

    Harassment of any kind is not tolerated. Harassment includes but is not limited to:

    • Verbal comments reinforcing social dominance based on gender, identity, orientation, disability, appearance, body size, race, age, or religion.
    • Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following.
    • Harassing photography or recording.
    • Sustained disruption of talks or other events.
    • Inappropriate physical contact.
    • Unwelcome sexual attention.
    • Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviour.

    Reporting

    If someone makes you or anyone else feel unsafe or unwelcome, please report it as soon as possible (ox.orchard@gmail.com or speak to a host in person). We take all reports very seriously.
